Your Consultation Overview

20/20 Vision

20/20 vision is a term used to express normal visual acuity (the clarity or sharpness of vision) measured at a distance of 20 feet. If you have 20/20 vision, you can see clearly at 20 feet what should normally be seen at that distance. If you have 20/100 vision, it means that you must be as close as 20 feet to see what a person with normal vision can see at 100 feet.

Myopia (Nearsightedness)

Nearsightedness or myopia is a condition in which close objects are seen clearly, but objects farther away appear blurred. This is because the eyeball is too long relative to the focusing power of the cornea and the lens of the eye, and the image falls in front of the retina. Myopia is a fairly common condition and one that can be corrected through the refractive surgery options available.

Astigmatism

Astigmatism occurs when the image of an object is not focused on the central part of the retina. This is a result of a cornea which is shaped like a cylinder rather than a sphere. This results in vision that is blurred at all distances but especially at far.

Hyperopia (Farsightedness)

Farsightedness is a condition in which distant objects may be seen relatively clearly, but close objects do not come into proper focus. This occurs when your eyeball is too short or the cornea is too flat. As a result, the light entering your eye is focused behind the retina. This condition can mimic or aggravate the need for reading glasses which is observed with the normal aging process.

Cataracts

Cataract is a natural aging process of the eye where the crystalline lens becomes progressively cloudy. During the cataract surgery, this cloudy lens is replaced with a specialized intraocular artificial lens restoring clear vision.

Keratoconus

Keratoconus is a condition in which the cornea becomes progressively weak. This leads to irregular shape of the cornea causing blurred vision. In the early phase, the vision can be corrected with either glasses or specialized contact lenses. However, in its advanced stages, surgery is required to restore vision.

Bladed Lasik

In bladed LASIK surgery, the corneal flap is created by using a device called Microkeratome. Once the flap is created with a blade, an excimer laser is used to change the curvature of your cornea to correct your vision.

Bladeless LASIK

In bladeless LASIK, the corneal flap is created using a high precision femtosecond laser. Once the flap is created, an excimer laser is used to change the curvature of your cornea to correct your vision. Its advantage include higher precision, customizable flap, and greater comfort.

PRK (Photorefractive keratectomy)

PRK (Photorefractive keratectomy) is a form of laser vision correction where reshaping of the cornea is achieved with an excimer laser without creating a flap. This procedure may be recommended for people with thin corneas.

ICL

Implantable Collamer Lens surgery is a procedure in which vision correction is accomplished by inserting a biocompatible and customized lens inside the eye. This reversible procedure is a great alternative to laser vision correction for patients with high correction or thin corneas.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is corrective eye surgery painful?

Before corrective eye surgery, we always administer anesthetic eye drops and other medications designed to ensure a calm, comfortable procedure. Most patients report that treatment is fast, easy, with little discomfort. After LASIK, patients experience mild discomfort during the early stages of recovery. But with rest and sleep, the next morning most people can see well enough to go to work the next day.

How long will it take me to recover?

Most patients notice improved vision the same day and are able to return to most normal activities the next day. Patients typically notice significant improvements over the first week, but it can take up to a month for full recovery. Every patient’s recovery from LASIK surgery is different. Some patients take longer to heal and experience improvements in vision and comfort over longer periods of time.

How good will my vision be after LASIK?

While it is difficult to predict the exact outcome of any procedure in advance, our patients reliably achieve excellent vision after surgery. Our results are simply clearer with an enhancement rate of less than 2% and a patient satisfaction rate of 98% .
